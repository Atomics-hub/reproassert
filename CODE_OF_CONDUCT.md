# ReproAssert Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

ReproAssert exists to make software evidence easier to inspect and challenge. We want the project to be a place where people can question a result, report a security concern, or admit uncertainty without being dismissed or attacked.

Everyone participating in the repository, reviews, issues, discussions, events, or other project spaces is expected to follow this code, regardless of experience, identity, role, employer, or relationship to the project.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and patient.
- Critique claims, code, methods, and evidence rather than people.
- State uncertainty and conflicts of interest plainly.
- Give credit for ideas, reports, tests, research, and fixes.
- Protect private information, credentials, unpublished vulnerabilities, and embargoed reports.
- Respect maintainers' time and project scope.
- Accept a clear decision and use the documented appeal or review path instead of escalating pressure.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Sexualized language or attention, unwelcome contact, or deliberate misgendering.
- Personal attacks, insults, trolling, dogpiling, or sustained disruption.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Coercing maintainers, reporters, or reviewers to change a technical or moderation decision.
- Retaliating against someone who raises a good-faith safety, security, or conduct concern.
- Knowingly submitting secrets, malicious payloads, or third-party private data outside an explicitly authorized security process.

## Technical disagreement

Strong disagreement is welcome when it improves the evidence. When challenging a result:

1. identify the exact claim or behavior;
2. provide a minimal reproduction, source, or falsifiable counterexample when possible;
3. separate observed facts from interpretation; and
4. avoid presenting benchmark targets, fixture results, or vendor claims as independent proof.

Maintainers may close repetitive, off-topic, unsafe, or non-actionable threads after explaining the reason.

## Scope and enforcement

This code applies in project spaces and when someone is publicly representing the project. Maintainers may edit or remove contributions and may warn, temporarily restrict, or permanently ban participants whose behavior violates this code. Enforcement should be proportionate, documented privately where appropriate, and focused on community safety rather than punishment.

## Reporting

For project conduct concerns, contact the repository owners through a private channel they publish. If no private moderation channel is available, open a minimal issue requesting private follow-up and include no sensitive personal details.

For credible security vulnerabilities, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). Do not include exploit details, secrets, or vulnerable third-party repository data in a public issue.

For urgent threats or platform-wide abuse, use [GitHub Support's abuse reporting process](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse).

Maintainers should acknowledge a report, limit access to people needed to assess it, avoid conflicts of interest, and communicate the outcome to the reporter when doing so is safe and lawful. Good-faith reporters will not be penalized for raising a concern that is ultimately not substantiated.
